How tractor loan EMI is calculated

EMI is calculated on the reducing balance method, the standard used by Indian banks and NBFCs for tractor loans: EMI = P × R × (1+R)N ÷ [(1+R)N − 1], where P is the loan principal, R is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12), and N is the number of monthly installments. If a SMAM subsidy applies, it reduces P directly — it does not change your interest rate, but a smaller loan amount means a smaller EMI and far less interest paid over the loan's life.

Why the subsidy toggle matters

Most EMI calculators assume you're financing the full purchase price. But many tractor buyers in India are eligible for a SMAM or state subsidy that's credited against the purchase before the loan is even sanctioned — meaning the loan principal, and therefore the EMI, is calculated on a smaller number than the sticker price. Toggling the subsidy on above shows you the real EMI you'd likely face after subsidy, not the inflated full-price EMI most calculators show.
