Buying guide · June 2026

2WD vs 4WD: which do you actually need?

4WD variants typically cost ₹1-1.5 lakh more than their 2WD counterparts in the same model line — the Swaraj 744 FE 4WD, for instance, runs roughly ₹1.3-1.4 lakh above the base 2WD version. That's a meaningful jump, and for most Indian farms, it doesn't pay for itself.

What 4WD actually changes

Four-wheel drive sends power to the front axle as well as the rear, which improves traction on wet, loose, or uneven ground and reduces wheel slippage when pulling heavy implements. It matters most in two situations: muddy or waterlogged fields (common in paddy-growing regions during certain seasons) and hilly or uneven terrain where a 2WD tractor's front wheels can lose grip on slopes.

Outside those conditions, the front-wheel assist mostly sits unused. On firm, level soil — the majority of cropland in India — a 2WD tractor with correctly inflated tyres and a properly weighted rear end gets equivalent traction for most tasks.

When 4WD is worth the extra cost

If your farm regularly works wet paddy fields, has slopes, or you frequently haul heavy loads (multi-tonne trailers, dense soil ploughing) where wheel slip becomes a recurring problem, the 4WD premium is a reasonable trade. It also tends to hold resale value slightly better, since fewer used 4WD units circulate in the market.

When 2WD is the better buy

For dry-land cropping, level fields, and standard implement work — cultivation, seed drilling, basic rotavator use — a 2WD tractor does the job at a lower upfront cost and, generally, lower maintenance cost over its life (fewer driveline components to service). Most first-time buyers on small-to-medium farms are better served putting the ₹1-1.5 lakh difference toward a better-specified implement instead.

A quick test

Ask yourself: in the last two seasons, how many times did wheel slip actually stop you from completing a task on time? If the honest answer is "rarely or never," 2WD is very likely the right call. If it's "regularly, especially in wet months," the 4WD premium will pay for itself in time saved.
